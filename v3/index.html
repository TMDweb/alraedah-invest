Why compare 7% effective with 4% flat?

A flat return treats your money as locked for the full period. An effective return accounts for when money comes back to you each month.

Selected view With the bank example, SAR 10,000 becomes SAR 10,400 at the end of the year. With Alraedah monthly distributions, about SAR 865 comes back each month. If those distributions are reinvested, the illustrative year-end value is about SAR 10,723.

Invest, receive, decide again.

The point is not just a higher number. The point is that your money is not waiting silently until the end of the period. Monthly distributions give you a choice: keep the cash, or put it back to work.

Questions investors ask first.

Short answers, based on the foundational explainer, with compliant language where the original Arabic needs legal-safe wording.

Is my capital guaranteed?

No. Alraedah has 10+ years of experience in the investment field, but returns are target-based and capital is exposed to risk.

Is the return really 7%?

It is a 7% effective target return. Because you receive capital plus target return monthly, the amount still invested decreases over time.

Why would I choose this over a 4% bank example?

The bank example pays at the end. Alraedah pays monthly. If you reinvest those monthly distributions, the illustrative outcome can be higher.

Why monthly payouts?

Monthly payouts make your money available during the year. You can keep the cash or reinvest it.

What is the minimum investment?

You can start from SAR 1,000.

Is Alraedah Invest regulated?

Yes. Alraedah is licensed and regulated by the Saudi Central Bank (SAMA), and opportunities are reviewed for Shariah compliance.
